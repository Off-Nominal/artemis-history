# Artemis — Roadmap

> Forward-looking plan as of March 26, 2026

## Upcoming Milestones

- **April 2026**: Artemis II — crewed lunar flyby (pending ICPS repair)
- **2027**: CLPS 2.0 robotic landings begin (up to 30 over 15 years)
- **Mid-2027**: Artemis III — LEO integrated systems test with commercial lander(s)
- **Early 2028**: Artemis IV — FIRST CREWED LUNAR LANDING
- **2028**: Lunar Terrain Vehicle initial operating capability
- **Late 2028**: SR-1 Freedom — first nuclear-powered interplanetary spacecraft to Mars
- **2028+**: Artemis V+ — one landing every six months using commercial reusable hardware
- **~2030**: Permanent lunar base near south pole (Phase 3)

## Lunar Base Phases

1. **"Build, Test, Learn"**: Accelerated CLPS robotic missions — rovers, instruments, drones
2. **"Establish Early Infrastructure"**: Semi-habitable facilities, JAXA pressurized rover
3. **"Enable Long-Duration Presence"**: ASI Multi-Purpose Habitats, CSA Lunar Utility Vehicle

## Current Program Cadence Target

One Artemis mission per year through Artemis V, then one landing every six months
using commercially procured reusable hardware. Standardized SLS Block 1.

## Open Competitions

- **Lunar lander**: SpaceX Starship HLS and Blue Origin Blue Moon competing — first ready wins
- **Spacesuits**: Axiom Space and Collins Aerospace (xEVAS contracts)
- **CLPS 2.0**: 10-year ordering period, $6B cap

## Gateway Status

Paused indefinitely as of March 24, 2026. Hardware being reallocated to SR-1 Freedom
Mars mission. Not formally canceled.

## Long-Term Vision

Land on the Moon by 2028. Build permanent lunar base by ~2030. Launch first
nuclear-powered spacecraft to Mars by end of 2028. One landing every six months.
61 nations signed on through the Artemis Accords. ~$20B committed over seven years.
