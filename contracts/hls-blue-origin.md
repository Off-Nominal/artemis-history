# Blue Origin Human Landing System Contract

## Sustaining Lunar Development Program

**Date**: May 19, 2023
**Value**: $3.4 billion (firm-fixed-price)
**Provider**: Blue Origin

NASA awards Blue Origin a contract to develop the Blue Moon Mark 2 (MK2)
crewed lunar lander as a second Human Landing System provider.

## Vehicle: Blue Moon MK2

- Two-stage lander (descent element + ascent element)
- Propellant: Liquid hydrogen and liquid oxygen (BE-7 engine)
- Reusable descent element
- Partners: Lockheed Martin, Draper, Boeing, Astrobotic, Honeybee Robotics

## Mission Assignment

- Intended for the **Artemis V** mission
- Provides competitive redundancy in the HLS program

## Program Structure

NASA now has two HLS providers:
1. **SpaceX Starship HLS** - Artemis III and IV
2. **Blue Origin Blue Moon MK2** - Artemis V and beyond

This dual-provider approach ensures competition and reduces single-point
dependency for sustained lunar surface access.
