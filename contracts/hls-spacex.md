# Human Landing System Contract Award

## SpaceX Starship HLS

**Date**: April 16, 2021
**Value**: $2.89 billion (firm-fixed-price)
**Provider**: SpaceX

NASA selected SpaceX as the sole provider for the Human Landing System
under the NextSTEP-2 Appendix H program.

## Scope

- One uncrewed demonstration landing on the lunar surface
- One crewed lunar landing (Artemis III)

## Vehicle: Starship HLS

SpaceX will develop a lunar-optimized variant of their Starship vehicle.

### Key Features
- Propellant: Liquid methane and liquid oxygen
- Crew capacity: Designed for large crews and cargo
- Reusability: Designed for multiple missions
- Lunar-specific modifications:
  - Solar arrays for extended surface operations
  - Modified RCS thrusters for lunar landing
  - Crew elevator for surface access
  - Enhanced communications and navigation

### Architecture
- Starship HLS launched to Earth orbit by Super Heavy booster
- Multiple tanker flights to refuel in orbit
- Transit to lunar orbit for crew transfer from Orion
- Descend to lunar surface with crew
- Ascend from surface and return crew to Orion

## Competitors Not Selected
- Blue Origin "National Team" (Blue Moon lander)
- Dynetics (DHLS)

Both teams filed bid protests with the GAO.
