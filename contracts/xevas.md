# Exploration Extravehicular Activity Services (xEVAS)

## Contract Award: June 1, 2022

NASA awards the xEVAS contracts worth up to $3.5 billion total to two
companies, transitioning from government-developed spacesuits to
commercially provided services.

## Awardees

### Axiom Space
- Developing the Axiom Extravehicular Mobility Unit (AxEMU)
- First task order: $228.5 million for Artemis III moonwalking suit
- Based in Houston, TX

### Collins Aerospace
- Developing next-generation EVA suit
- Focused on ISS EVA services initially

## Background

NASA's in-house xEMU (Exploration Extravehicular Mobility Unit) spacesuit
development experienced significant delays and cost overruns. A 2021 NASA
Office of Inspector General report found the xEMU suits would not be ready
until April 2025 at the earliest, years behind schedule.

The xEVAS contracts shift responsibility to the commercial sector,
allowing NASA to purchase EVA services rather than developing hardware.

## Suit Requirements
- Lunar surface operations in south pole conditions
- Enhanced mobility and dexterity
- Compatibility with diverse crew member body types
- Extended EVA duration capability
- Dust mitigation features
