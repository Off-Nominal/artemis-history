# EM-1 Crew Decision: Remain Uncrewed

**Date:** May 12, 2017
**Decision by:** Acting Administrator Robert Lightfoot

## Outcome

NASA has decided to keep Exploration Mission 1 (EM-1) as an uncrewed flight.

## Lightfoot Statement

> "After evaluating cost, risk, and technical factors in a project of this
> magnitude, it is difficult to accommodate changes needed for a crewed EM-1
> mission at this time."

## Findings

The feasibility study found that adding crew to EM-1 was **technically possible**
but would have required:

- **$600–900 million** in additional funding
- Significant schedule delay (pushing EM-1 into the 2020s)
- Numerous safety waivers for systems not designed for crew on the first flight
- Accelerating life support system integration by approximately three years

## Rationale

NASA cited **cost as the primary factor**, not safety. The additional funding
would have required a contentious budget fight, and the schedule delays would
have undermined the goal of accelerating crewed spaceflight.

## Impact

EM-1 will proceed as planned: an uncrewed SLS/Orion test flight around the Moon.
The first crewed mission remains EM-2, targeted for the early 2020s. The decision
preserves the existing development timeline and avoids the risk of a first-flight
crew on an unproven vehicle.
