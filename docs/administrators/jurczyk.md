# Steve Jurczyk — Acting NASA Administrator

**Term:** January 20, 2021 – May 3, 2021
**Status:** Acting Administrator

## Background

- NASA Associate Administrator (highest-ranking civil servant)
- Career NASA engineer, former director of Langley Research Center
- Led NASA's Space Technology Mission Directorate

## Key Actions

- Oversaw the **SpaceX HLS contract award** (April 16, 2021) — the $2.89 billion
  contract selecting SpaceX Starship as the Artemis lunar lander
- Managed the initial fallout when Blue Origin and Dynetics filed bid protests
  with the GAO (April 26, 2021)

## Context

Jurczyk's brief tenure included one of the most consequential contract decisions
in the Artemis program: selecting a single HLS provider (SpaceX) rather than the
two providers NASA had originally planned. This decision, driven by congressional
funding levels well below NASA's request, shaped the competitive dynamics of the
program for years to come and led to protests, lawsuits, and eventually a second
provider (Blue Origin) being added in 2023.
