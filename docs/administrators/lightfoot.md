# Robert M. Lightfoot Jr. — Acting NASA Administrator

**Term:** January 20, 2017 – April 23, 2018
**Status:** Acting Administrator

## Background

- NASA Associate Administrator (highest-ranking civil servant at NASA)
- Career NASA engineer, former director of Marshall Space Flight Center
- Became the longest-serving acting/interim administrator in NASA history at that time

## Key Actions

- Led NASA when President Trump signed **Space Policy Directive 1** (December 11, 2017),
  which formally redirected NASA's human exploration program from an asteroid-first
  approach back to the Moon as a stepping stone to Mars
- Continued SLS and Orion development through Critical Design Reviews
- Navigated the SLS Block 1 vs. Block 1B configuration debates
- Managed the transition from Obama-era "Journey to Mars" to Trump-era lunar focus

## Context

Lightfoot's 15-month tenure as acting administrator was one of the most consequential
interim periods in NASA history. SPD-1 fundamentally reoriented NASA's human exploration
program and set the stage for what would soon be named "Artemis."
