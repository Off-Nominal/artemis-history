# Charles F. Bolden Jr. — 12th NASA Administrator

**Term:** July 17, 2009 – January 20, 2017
**Status:** Administrator (Senate-confirmed)
**Appointed by:** President Barack Obama

## Background

- Retired Marine Corps Major General
- Former astronaut: four Space Shuttle missions including piloting the flight that
  deployed the Hubble Space Telescope (STS-31) and commanding STS-60
- First African American to serve as permanent NASA Administrator
- Confirmed by Senate July 15, 2009

## Key Actions

- Managed the **cancellation of the Constellation program** (February 2010) following
  the Augustine Committee's findings
- Oversaw the **NASA Authorization Act of 2010**, which created:
  - **Space Launch System (SLS)** — the heavy-lift rocket
  - **Orion Multi-Purpose Crew Vehicle (MPCV)** — continuing the Orion capsule
- Managed the final Space Shuttle flights (last: STS-135, July 2011)
- Oversaw early SLS/Orion development milestones
- Led NASA during **Orion EFT-1** (December 5, 2014) — first test flight
- Navigated the Asteroid Redirect Mission era and Journey to Mars strategy
- Longest-serving administrator since James Webb

## Legacy for Artemis

Bolden's tenure created the two vehicles — SLS and Orion — that form the backbone
of the Artemis program. Though the destination changed multiple times during his
administration (asteroid, Mars, eventually back to Moon), the hardware development
continued and provided the foundation for Artemis.
