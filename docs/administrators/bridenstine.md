# Jim Bridenstine — 13th NASA Administrator

**Term:** April 23, 2018 – January 20, 2021
**Status:** Administrator (Senate-confirmed)
**Appointed by:** President Donald Trump (1st term)

## Background

- Former U.S. Representative from Oklahoma's 1st congressional district (2013–2018)
- Former Navy fighter pilot and combat veteran (flew E-2C Hawkeyes)
- MBA from Cornell University
- First member of Congress to serve as NASA Administrator
- Confirmed by Senate in a 50-49 party-line vote (April 19, 2018) — the closest
  confirmation vote for a NASA administrator in history

## Key Actions

- **Named the Artemis program** (May 14, 2019) — chose the name of Apollo's twin
  sister in Greek mythology to represent the return to the Moon
- Set the ambitious goal of landing the **first woman and next man** on the Moon
  by 2024, per Vice President Pence's March 2019 directive
- Created and signed the **Artemis Accords** (October 13, 2020) with 8 founding
  nations, establishing principles for peaceful lunar exploration
- Added the **Lunar Gateway** to the program architecture
- Issued the **HLS solicitation** (NextSTEP-2 Appendix H) for commercial lunar landers
- Announced the **18-member Artemis astronaut team** (December 2020)
- Oversaw the SLS Green Run hot fire test campaign at Stennis Space Center
- Managed NASA through the COVID-19 pandemic

## Legacy

Bridenstine transformed a hardware development program into a branded, international
endeavor with clear goals and commercial partnerships. The Artemis name, Artemis
Accords, and commercial lander strategy all originated during his tenure. He is
widely credited with building political support for the program across both parties.
