# Michael D. Griffin — 11th NASA Administrator

**Term:** April 14, 2005 – January 20, 2009
**Status:** Administrator (Senate-confirmed)
**Appointed by:** President George W. Bush

## Background

- Aerospace engineer with seven degrees including a PhD in aerospace engineering
- Former NASA Chief Engineer and Associate Administrator for Exploration
- Previously worked at Johns Hopkins Applied Physics Laboratory, In-Q-Tel, and SDI
- Considered one of the most technically qualified administrators in NASA history

## Key Actions

- Ordered the **Exploration Systems Architecture Study (ESAS)**, which redesigned
  NASA's approach to implementing the Vision for Space Exploration
- Formally launched the **Constellation program** (2005):
  - **Ares I** crew launch vehicle
  - **Ares V** cargo launch vehicle
  - **Orion** crew exploration vehicle (same spacecraft that flies today on SLS)
  - **Altair** lunar lander
- Managed the final years of Space Shuttle operations alongside Constellation development
- Set an initial lunar return target of 2020

## Legacy for Artemis

The Orion spacecraft Griffin commissioned survived Constellation's cancellation and
became the crew vehicle for the Space Launch System and Artemis program. Many of
the engineering decisions made during Griffin's tenure directly shaped the hardware
flying today.
