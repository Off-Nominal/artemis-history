# Sean Duffy — Acting NASA Administrator

**Term:** July 9, 2025 – December 17, 2025
**Status:** Acting Administrator (serving simultaneously as Secretary of Transportation)

## Background

- U.S. Secretary of Transportation (confirmed January 28, 2025)
- Former U.S. Representative from Wisconsin's 7th congressional district (2010–2019)
- Former Fox Business Network co-host
- Former county district attorney in Ashland County, Wisconsin
- Named Acting NASA Administrator via presidential announcement on Truth Social
- No aerospace or science background — drew public commentary as a non-traditional
  choice for NASA leadership

## Key Actions

### HLS Recompete (October 2025)

Duffy's most consequential decision was announcing that NASA would **open up the
Artemis III HLS lander contract to competition** beyond SpaceX alone.

- **October 20, 2025**: Announced on CNBC "Squawk Box" and Fox News that NASA
  would not wait for one company and would open competition
- Cited SpaceX Starship being behind schedule and the race against China's 2030
  lunar landing goal as primary motivations
- Directed both SpaceX and Blue Origin to submit acceleration proposals by October 29
- Planned to issue an RFI to the broader aerospace industry
- Highlighted Blue Origin's simpler **Blue Moon MK1** as a potential alternative
  that does not require orbital refueling

### Musk Confrontation

- Elon Musk attacked Duffy on X/Twitter (October 21, 2025), calling him "Sean Dummy"
  and questioning his qualifications
- Duffy responded diplomatically: "Love the passion. The race to the Moon is ON.
  Great companies shouldn't be afraid of a challenge."
- Former administrators Bridenstine and Bolden publicly supported the recompete
  at the American Astronautical Society's von Braun Symposium

## Legacy

Duffy's recompete decision, though controversial, accelerated HLS development and
led directly to the restructured Artemis architecture announced by Isaacman in
February 2026. The competitive pressure he introduced pushed both SpaceX and Blue
Origin to propose simplified, faster mission architectures.
