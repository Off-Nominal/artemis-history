# Augustine Committee Findings

## Review of U.S. Human Spaceflight Plans (September 2009)

The Review of U.S. Human Spaceflight Plans Committee (Augustine Committee)
published its findings after a comprehensive review of NASA's human
spaceflight program.

## Key Conclusions

- The Constellation Program as planned is not executable within the
  projected budget
- Under current funding profiles, a return to the Moon would not occur
  until the late 2020s at the earliest
- NASA's budget is insufficient for any exploration program that
  requires development of new launch vehicles
- The Committee presented several alternative architectures including
  a "Flexible Path" approach

## Options Presented

1. Program of Record (Constellation as-is) - underfunded
2. Constellation modified - extended ISS, delayed Moon
3. Flexible Path - visit near-Earth objects, lunar orbit, Mars orbit
   before surface missions
4. Moon First variants with commercial crew to LEO

## Impact

These findings cast serious doubt on the viability of the Constellation
Program under existing budget constraints.
