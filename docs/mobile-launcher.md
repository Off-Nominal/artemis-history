# Mobile Launcher Program

## Mobile Launcher 1 (ML-1)

### History
- Originally built for Constellation/Ares I: $234 million
- 2011-2012: Initial estimate for SLS modifications: $54 million
- March 2014: Formal estimate balloons to $384.7 million (7x increase)
- January 2020: Cost reaches $693 million

### OIG Report IG-20-013 (March 17, 2020)

"Audit of NASA's Development of Its Mobile Launchers"

Key findings:
- Total ML-1 investment approaching ~$927 million (including original build)
- $308 million over the March 2014 estimate
- Running 3+ years behind schedule
- Root cause: immature SLS requirements caused repeated design changes
- Design errors during tower outfitting, cabling conflicts, structural issues
- NASA spent $1.8M just to reroute cryogenic piping that conflicted with
  environmental control ducting

### SLS Cost (OIG Report IG-20-012, March 2020)
Separately, the OIG found NASA had spent $14.8 billion developing SLS
as of December 2019, versus the original $9.7 billion KDP-C commitment.
A 53% cost overrun.

## Mobile Launcher 2 (ML-2) - For SLS Block 1B

- Contractor: Bechtel National (awarded 2019)
- Original estimate: $383 million, completion March 2023
- By 2022: OIG projects $960 million, completion no earlier than 2026
- By 2024: OIG estimates up to $2.7 billion, ready no earlier than spring 2029
