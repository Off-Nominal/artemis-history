# Journey to Mars: Pioneering Next Steps in Space Exploration

## Published: October 8, 2015

**NASA Administrator**: Charles Bolden

NASA publishes a 36-page report outlining its strategy for human
exploration of Mars, establishing "Journey to Mars" as the organizing
framework for human spaceflight.

## Three Phases

### Phase 1: Earth Reliant (ongoing through ~2024)
- Research aboard the International Space Station
- Validate deep space technologies (life support, radiation mitigation)
- Develop commercial crew and cargo to LEO
- Understand effects of long-duration spaceflight on the human body

### Phase 2: Proving Ground (~2020s)
- Missions in cislunar space using SLS and Orion
- Asteroid Redirect Mission (crewed visit to captured boulder)
- Deep space habitat testing
- Demonstrate systems needed for Mars (solar electric propulsion, etc.)

### Phase 3: Earth Independent (~2030s)
- Missions to Mars vicinity (orbit, Phobos/Deimos)
- Eventually: crewed landing on Mars surface
- Establish sustained human presence

## Notable Omissions

The report does NOT include:
- Specific dates for Mars missions (beyond "the 2030s")
- Detailed cost estimates
- Concrete technical milestones with dates
- A funded plan for surface systems

Criticized as aspirational rather than actionable. Sometimes called
"PowerPoint to Mars" by detractors.
