# NASA Human Exploration Destination History

This document tracks the major strategic destination pivots in NASA's
human spaceflight program.

## January 14, 2004 - Vision for Space Exploration
**Destination**: Moon by 2020, then Mars (no date)
**Decision-maker**: President George W. Bush
Moon as stepping stone; Mars as long-term aspiration with no timeline.

## 2005 - Constellation Program
**Destination**: Moon by 2020
Formal program (Ares I, Ares V, Orion, Altair) to implement the Vision.

## February 1, 2010 - FY2011 Budget: NO DESTINATION

**Decision-maker**: President Barack Obama

The FY2011 budget request proposes canceling the entire Constellation
Program and replacing it with technology development investments and
commercial crew transportation to LEO.

Critically, the proposal contains **no specific destination beyond
low-Earth orbit**. No Moon. No asteroid. No Mars date. Just R&D.

This represents the most radical pivot in the program's history --
for approximately 10 weeks, NASA's human exploration program has no
stated deep-space destination.

The proposal draws intense criticism from Congress and Apollo-era
astronauts, including Neil Armstrong, Jim Lovell, and Gene Cernan,
who write an open letter calling it "devastating" to U.S. leadership
in space.
