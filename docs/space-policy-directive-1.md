# Space Policy Directive-1

## Signed: December 11, 2017

President Donald Trump signed Space Policy Directive-1 (SPD-1) at the
White House, formally directing NASA to lead the return of humans to
the Moon, followed by missions to Mars and beyond.

## Key Text

SPD-1 amends the National Space Policy to state:

"Lead an innovative and sustainable program of exploration with
commercial and international partners to enable human expansion
across the solar system and to bring back to Earth new knowledge and
opportunities. Beginning with missions beyond low-Earth orbit, the
United States will lead the return of humans to the Moon for long-term
exploration and utilization, followed by human missions to Mars and
other destinations."

## Significance

- Formally redirects NASA from the "Journey to Mars" focus of the Obama era
  back to a Moon-first approach
- Emphasizes commercial and international partnerships
- Sets the stage for what will become the Artemis program
- Builds on the National Space Council, reestablished by executive order
  on June 30, 2017, chaired by Vice President Mike Pence
