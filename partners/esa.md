# European Space Agency (ESA) Partnership

## European Service Module (ESM)

In December 2012, NASA and ESA reached an agreement for ESA to build the
European Service Module for the Orion spacecraft, starting with EM-1.

### Contractor
- Airbus Defence and Space, Bremen, Germany (prime contractor)

### Design Heritage
- Based on ESA's Automated Transfer Vehicle (ATV) technology
- ATV successfully flew five resupply missions to the ISS (2008-2015)

### Capabilities
- Primary propulsion: One Orbital Maneuvering System (OMS) engine
  (modified Space Shuttle OMS-E, ~6,000 lbs thrust)
- 8 auxiliary thrusters and 24 attitude control engines
- Four solar array wings generating ~11 kW
- Consumable storage: water, oxygen, nitrogen
- Thermal control for the crew module

### ESM-1
- For Exploration Mission-1 (uncrewed)
- Delivered to NASA's Kennedy Space Center: late 2018

This partnership represents Europe's critical contribution to human
deep space exploration and a continuation of transatlantic space cooperation.
