# Artemis Accords

## Signed: October 13, 2020

The Artemis Accords establish a practical set of principles to guide
cooperation among nations participating in NASA's Artemis program for
the civil exploration and peaceful use of the Moon, Mars, and other
celestial bodies.

## Founding Signatories (8 nations)

1. United States
2. Australia
3. Canada
4. Italy
5. Japan
6. Luxembourg
7. United Arab Emirates
8. United Kingdom

## Core Principles

1. **Peaceful purposes** - Consistent with the Outer Space Treaty
2. **Transparency** - Public description of policies and plans
3. **Interoperability** - Common standards for systems
4. **Emergency assistance** - Render aid to those in distress
5. **Registration of space objects** - Per the Registration Convention
6. **Scientific data release** - Public release of scientific data
7. **Heritage preservation** - Preserve outer space heritage sites
8. **Space resources** - Extraction and use per the Outer Space Treaty
9. **Deconfliction of activities** - Avoid harmful interference
10. **Orbital debris mitigation** - Responsible disposal of spacecraft

## Significance

The Accords provide a framework for international cooperation in lunar
exploration outside of a formal treaty process, building on existing
international space law.
