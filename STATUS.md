# Artemis — Program Status

> As of March 26, 2026

**NASA Administrator:** Jared Isaacman (15th Administrator)
**Goal:** Permanent lunar base near south pole by ~2030; first landing 2028 (Artemis IV)
**Next Milestone:** Artemis II crewed flyby (April 2026); Artemis III LEO systems test (2027); Artemis IV landing (early 2028)

## Vehicles
- **Space Launch System (SLS): Block 1 only — Block 1B, Block 2, and ML-2 canceled**
- **Orion MPCV: Heat shield root cause identified — AVCOAT permeability; fixes being implemented**
- **Human Landing System: Open competition — first company ready gets the Artemis IV landing**
- **Lunar Gateway: PAUSED INDEFINITELY — hardware being reallocated to SR-1 Freedom Mars mission**

## Ignition Event (March 24, 2026)
Administrator Isaacman unveiled "Ignition," a sweeping agencywide realignment:
- **Lunar base**: ~$20B over seven years, three-phase plan for permanent south pole base
- **Mission cadence**: One landing every six months after Artemis V, using commercial reusable hardware
- **SR-1 Freedom**: First nuclear-powered interplanetary spacecraft, launching to Mars by end of 2028
- **CLPS 2.0**: Up to 30 robotic landings starting 2027, $6B cap over 15 years
- **LEO transition**: ISS extended to mid-2030s; new government Core Module for post-ISS ecosystem
- **Gateway paused**: Resources redirected to Mars and lunar base priorities

## International Partners
Artemis Accords: 61 signatory nations

## Overview
Artemis II targets April 2026 after ICPS repair. Starship HLS and Blue Moon development continue under open competition. Gateway paused indefinitely — hardware reallocated to SR-1 Freedom Mars mission. 61 nations have signed the Accords. The program has been restructured: Artemis III is an LEO test, first landing moves to Artemis IV (2028). NASA targets a permanent lunar base by ~2030 and one landing every six months thereafter.
