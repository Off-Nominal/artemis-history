# Artemis I

## MISSION COMPLETE - SUCCESS

### Launch
- **Date**: November 16, 2022, 1:47 AM EST (06:47 UTC)
- **Vehicle**: SLS Block 1 + Orion + ESM-1
- **Launch Site**: LC-39B, Kennedy Space Center
- **Result**: First flight of the integrated SLS/Orion system

### Mission Timeline

| Flight Day | Date | Event |
|-----------|------|-------|
| Day 1 | Nov 16 | Launch, TLI burn, solar array deployment |
| Day 2 | Nov 17 | First outbound trajectory correction |
| Day 6 | Nov 21 | Powered lunar flyby (~80 mi altitude) |
| Day 10 | Nov 25 | Distant retrograde orbit insertion |
| Day 13 | Nov 28 | Maximum distance from Earth: 268,563 mi |
| Day 16 | Dec 1 | DRO departure burn |
| Day 20 | Dec 5 | Return powered lunar flyby |
| Day 26 | Dec 11 | Reentry and splashdown |

### Key Achievements

- **Distance record**: Orion reached 268,563 miles (432,210 km) from Earth,
  the farthest distance traveled by a spacecraft designed for human occupancy
- **Mission duration**: 25.5 days (25 days, 10 hours, 53 minutes)
- **SLS performance**: Exceeded expectations on first flight
- **Orion systems**: Life support, propulsion, navigation all nominal
- **European Service Module**: Performed flawlessly
- **Heat shield**: Withstood reentry temperatures of ~5,000 degrees F
- **Recovery**: USS Portland recovered capsule in Pacific Ocean near
  Guadalupe Island, west of Baja California

### Secondary Payloads
10 CubeSats deployed during transit, including:
- BioSentinel (biology experiment in deep space)
- LunaH-Map (lunar hydrogen mapper)
- NEA Scout (solar sail asteroid mission)
- Various technology demonstrations

### Pre-Launch History
- Wet dress rehearsal: 4 attempts (Apr-Jun 2022)
- Launch attempt 1 (Aug 29): Scrubbed - engine temperature
- Launch attempt 2 (Sep 3): Scrubbed - hydrogen leak
- Rollback to VAB, weathered Hurricane Ian, repairs
- Rollback to pad: November 4, 2022

## Significance

Artemis I validates the integrated SLS/Orion system for deep space
operations, clearing the path for Artemis II (first crewed flight)
and Artemis III (lunar landing).

## Mission Result: FULL SUCCESS

Artemis I concluded on December 11, 2022 when Orion splashed down in the
Pacific Ocean west of Baja California near Guadalupe Island at 12:40 PM EST.

The USS Portland recovered the capsule. Total mission duration: 25 days,
10 hours, 53 minutes.

All primary mission objectives achieved. SLS and Orion performed beyond
expectations, validating the system for crewed flights.
