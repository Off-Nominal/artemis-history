# Exploration Flight Test-1 (EFT-1)

## Mission Summary

- **Date**: December 5, 2014
- **Vehicle**: Orion crew module (without European Service Module)
- **Launch Vehicle**: Delta IV Heavy
- **Launch Site**: Cape Canaveral, SLC-37B
- **Duration**: 4 hours, 24 minutes
- **Orbits**: 2

## Mission Profile

EFT-1 was Orion's first flight, designed to test critical systems needed
for deep space exploration.

- Orbit 1: ~100 nautical miles altitude (parking orbit)
- Orbit 2: Apogee of approximately 3,600 miles (5,800 km) - 15x higher
  than the International Space Station
- Reentry speed: ~20,000 mph (8.9 km/s)
- Heat shield temperature: up to 4,000 degrees F (2,200 C)
- Successful parachute deployment and splashdown in the Pacific Ocean

## Key Tests

- Heat shield performance at lunar-return-like speeds
- Parachute system deployment sequence
- Jettison of launch abort system fairing and service module fairing
- Avionics and flight software in deep space radiation environment
- Separation events

## Result

FULL SUCCESS. All test objectives met or exceeded. Heat shield performed
as designed. Orion declared ready to proceed toward EM-1 integration.
